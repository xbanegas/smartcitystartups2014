Details

The first gathering to explore how we can help startups solving our biggest urban challenges surrounding sustainable buildings, mobility, governance and service delivery. The event is limited to 100 participants to maximize interaction during the meet-up.


Over one and a half days, participants will share their perspectives on current urban issues and potential solutions. Representatives from startups, investors, foundations, researchers and cities will have a chance to engage in dialogue contributing their expertise on the issues. Promising cases and strategies will be documented and subsequently shared publicly, inviting participants to continue their ideation online.

For Smart City Startups 2014, we will explore 3 main themes:

1 - Partnerships

From superior domain and policy expertise, to local business community connections, cities have a lot to offer startups. In the beginning stages, startups typically struggle with issues such as acquiring customers (for feedback and testing) and finding funding. How can cities support startups in these areas?

2 - Procurement

Startups often struggle to get their first pilots and reference customers, which discourages many entrepreneurs and investors from pursuing opportunities that involve selling to cities. What can cities and startups do to navigate current procurement processes and improve them?

3 - Funding

Traditional venture firms are still a critical part of venture financing, however a number of options are quickly emerging including crowdfunding, corporate venture firms, city accelerators, foundations, non-profits in sectors ranging from IT to energy. How should startups think about these options? What can cities do to support access to these expanding private funding options?

FAQ



What costs, if any, are associated with attending the conference?

We are not charging attendees, so costs are primarily travel to Miami.

What opportunities exist to meet with investors or social entrepreneurs?

During the day, most of the sessions will be workshops in groups of 10 or fewer. For each session, the groups will change. In the evening there will be a "demo crawl" during which startups showcase what they are working on and drinks will be available.

Do attendees need to prepare an investor deck or come with a functional prototype to the conference?

There will not be any pitching. This is not a fundraising event, but an event focused on exploring how we can support startups working to solve urban challenges facing issues of funding, procurement and partnerships.

What oppurtunity to connect with potential customers and city officials regarding pilots and other collaborations will there be?

The first evening of the event will be an opportunity for you to demo your product and engage with attendees (city officials, potential customers, and investors) on a one on one level.

What are attendees expected to bring to the conference as a value-added contribution?

Attendees are expected to have experience dealing with startups, cities, and or investors.

What are the objectives of the event?

There are a few :
  • build more connections among people interested in the smart city startup space (measured by ongoing conversations, which we will encourage and host when possible)
  • creating new city activities focused on startups that want to help the cities (vs startups in general) - anything from working with specific startups to broader initiatives
  • more interest from entrepreneurs and investors in the space
